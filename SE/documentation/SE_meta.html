Methodological Appendix: Sweden

For Sweden, I compile a comprehensive panel of housing statistics using register-based data from Statistics Sweden (SCB). All datasets are extracted through the SCB Statistikdatabasen interface and cover the full set of 290 municipalities. The primary variables include the number of dwellings, their characteristics (type of building, size, period of construction), the distribution of residential and non-residential floor space, income data at the municipal level, house prices and rents.

For the price data, information is collected directly from the source website at the municipal level (Statistics Sweden). The dataset includes the municipality code and name, the number of purchases, the average transaction price (expressed in SEK thousands), and percentage changes in prices over multiple horizons: one year (2023–2024), five years (2019–2024), ten years (2014–2024), and twenty years (2004–2024). To obtain a complete time series, the missing year is interpolated using regional House Price Indices (HPI), including indices for the main cities. In addition, linear interpolation is applied to ensure consistency and to construct a smooth municipal-level price time series.

1. Dwelling Stock by Useful Floor Space, Building Type, and Municipality (2013–2024)

Source: Number of dwellings by region, type of building and useful floor space.
Table: BO0104T5
Link: SCB BO0104T5

We extract the annual number of dwellings by municipality, disaggregated by:

  • Type of building:
    • One- or two-dwelling buildings: Detached one- and two-dwelling units, semi-detached houses, row houses, and linked buildings. Seasonal or secondary-use buildings are excluded.
    • Multi-dwelling buildings: Buildings with three or more apartments, including balcony access housing.
    • Other buildings: Non-residential buildings that nevertheless contain ordinary dwellings (e.g., commercial or public buildings with residential units).
  • Useful floor space (sqm categories): <31; 31–40; 41–50; 51–60; 61–70; 71–80; 81–90; 91–100; 101–110; 111–120; 121–130; 131–140; 141–150; 151–160; 161–170; 171–180; 181–190; 191–200; >200

2. Dwelling Stock by Number of Rooms and Kitchen Type (2013–2024)

Source: Number of dwellings by region, type of building and size of dwelling.
Table: BO0104T09
Link: SCB BO0104T09

  • Types of building: Multi-dwelling buildings, other buildings, and special housing.
  • Dwelling layout categories:
    • Dwellings without kitchen equipment
    • 1 room and kitchen
    • 1 room and kitchenette
    • 2 rooms and kitchen
    • 2 or more rooms with kitchenette

Note: One- or two-dwelling buildings are not included in this dataset.

3. Dwelling Stock by Period of Construction (2013–2019)

Source: Number of dwellings by region, type of building and period of construction.
Table: BO0104T02
Link: SCB BO0104T02

  • Type of building: One- or two-dwelling buildings, multi-dwelling buildings, and other buildings.
  • Construction period: Ten-year intervals from pre-1930, 1931–1940, …, 2011–2020. Data for 2021 onward are not available.

6. Average Monthly Salary by Municipality (2007–2024)

Source: Average monthly salary in the municipalities by region, sex, observations and year.
Link: SCB AM0106A

  • Variables:
    • Average monthly salary in the municipalities (212 municipalities for number of employees and 290 for salary)
    • Number of employees by sex: men, women, and total
  • Time coverage: 2007–2024
  • Population: Employees aged 18–68 (until 2013, upper age limit 64; from 2023, upper age limit increased from 66 to 68)
  • Definition: Monthly salary refers to contractually negotiated salaries, including base salary and fixed supplements, as well as variable supplements such as overtime, on-call, and preparation compensations.

8. Average Prices for One- and Two-Dwelling Buildings by Municipality (2024)

Source: Average prices for one- and two-dwelling buildings by municipality in 2024.
Link: SCB Average Prices 2024

The table contains:

  • Number of purchases and average prices for one- and two-dwelling buildings by municipality in 2024.
  • Development of prices over different periods:
    • 1 year: 2023–2024
    • 5 years: 2019–2024
    • 10 years: 2014–2024
